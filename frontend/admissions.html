Who We Admit

Open to All Children Ready to Learn

Cornerstone Schools admits children from Baby Class (age 3) through Primary Seven (age ~13). Entry at all levels is subject to availability of places and a brief assessment appropriate to the child's age.

We do not discriminate on the basis of religion, ethnicity, or socioeconomic background. We also maintain a scholarship programme — see below.

Applications close on 31 October for January intake and 31 March for May intake. Early application is strongly advised as places fill quickly.

Age Entry Guide

Baby Class Age 3+
Middle Class Age 4+
Top Class Age 5+
Primary One Age 6+
Primary Two – Six Age 7–12
Primary Seven Age 12–13

How to Apply

A Simple Four-Step Process

01

Submit an Enquiry

Complete the online enquiry form below or visit the school office in person. Our admissions team will respond within 48 hours to confirm your interest and advise on availability for your child's preferred class level.

02

School Visit & Assessment

We invite prospective families for a guided school tour and a short, informal assessment appropriate to the child's age — this is not an exam, but a warm opportunity for us to understand the child's current level and needs.

03

Offer & Acceptance

Successful applicants receive a formal offer letter. To secure the place, parents are asked to pay a non-refundable registration fee and submit the required documents (birth certificate, previous school reports where applicable, immunisation records).

04

Welcome to Cornerstone

On the first day of term, every new pupil is welcomed by their class teacher and introduced to the Cornerstone community. Our transition programme ensures every child settles in quickly, confidently, and with joy.

Financial Support

The Cornerstone Scholarship Programme

We believe that financial circumstances should never be a barrier to a quality education. Each academic year, Cornerstone awards a limited number of partial and full scholarships to academically deserving pupils from low-income families.

Scholarship applications are assessed on a combination of academic potential, family financial need, and character. To apply, contact our office directly.
